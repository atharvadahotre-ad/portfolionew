01 — Context

Three teams, zero shared language

Orbits was scaling fast. Three product teams were shipping independently — each using their own component library, their own spacing scale, and their own interpretation of the brand. The result was a product that looked fractured and felt inconsistent.

I was handed a brief with three deliverables: a unified design system, a redesigned core app experience, and a marketing landing page to drive the v2 waitlist. Six weeks. One designer.

The Brief
"Give us one visual language. Make the app feel like one product. Then sell it to the world."
02 — Design System

Building the foundation first

I started with tokens — colour, typography, spacing, radius, and shadow. Everything downstream would inherit from these primitives. I ran a two-day audit of every existing UI screen across all three teams to catalogue what existed and identify what needed to be standardised.

The system was built in Figma with Auto Layout, component properties, and interactive states for every component. I wrote a companion documentation page in Notion covering usage guidelines, anti-patterns, and handoff notes for engineers.

03 — Process

Parallel tracks, tight feedback loops

Week 1–2
Audit & Token Architecture
Inventoried 300+ screens, extracted patterns, and built the token system in Figma Variables. Aligned with engineering on naming conventions.
Week 2–4
Component Library Build
Built 200+ components across atoms, molecules, and organisms. Every component had 4 states: default, hover, active, disabled.
Week 3–5
App Redesign
Rebuilt the core app flows using the new system — dashboard, analytics, onboarding, and settings. Tested critical flows with 4 users.
Week 5–6
Landing Page & Launch
Designed and built the marketing landing page in Framer. Iterated on the hero and social proof sections 3 times based on team feedback.
04 — App & Landing Page

One product, one visual voice

With the system in place, redesigning the app was faster than expected. The hardest part wasn't the design — it was the negotiation. Each team had preferences and legacy patterns they were attached to. I facilitated three design reviews and used the system documentation to make decisions objective rather than opinion-based.

05 — Results

Shipped in 6 weeks. Still running.

The landing page went live on a Tuesday. By Friday it had 2,000+ waitlist signups — primarily from organic Twitter shares of the design system preview. The system became the single source of truth across all three product teams and has been maintained and extended ever since.

The engineering team reported that component handoff time dropped by an estimated 40% compared to previous projects. The design system documentation was cited as a major factor in the quality and speed of implementation.

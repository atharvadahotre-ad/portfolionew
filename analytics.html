01 — The Problem

Data everywhere. Insight nowhere.

The platform had powerful data — but it was locked behind 47-column spreadsheet exports, deeply nested navigation, and a dashboard that required users to run a report before they could see anything meaningful. Power users had developed elaborate workarounds. New users were churning within their first 30 days.

The business problem was clear: enterprise accounts were underutilising the platform, leading to poor renewal conversations. The product team needed a redesign that would make the value of the platform immediately visible — without reducing its analytical depth.

Core Tension
"Power users needed depth. New users needed clarity. The design had to serve both without compromising either."
02 — Research

Understanding two very different mental models

I ran 8 contextual interviews — split evenly between power users and newer users. I watched them use the existing platform and asked them to narrate their thinking. The gap was stark: power users were navigating from memory, while new users were constantly lost.

FullStory session data confirmed what the interviews suggested — 68% of new users never made it past the first dashboard. The primary navigation was being used in completely different orders by the two user groups.

03 — Design Process

From information architecture
to polished interface

Phase 1
IA Restructure
Ran a card sort with 12 users to rebuild the information architecture from user mental models rather than database structure. Reduced top-level nav items from 11 to 5.
Phase 2
Progressive Disclosure
Designed a layered system: summary cards at the surface, drill-down tables on click, full export on demand. Every level has a clear purpose and a clear escape.
Phase 3
Intelligent Defaults
Built a personalised dashboard that surfaces the 3 metrics each user cares about most based on their role and usage history. No configuration required on day one.
Phase 4
Visualisation System
Designed a consistent chart library — 8 chart types with clear usage guidance. Replaced 12 inconsistent legacy chart styles with one unified visual language.
04 — Solution

Surface the signal. Hide the noise.

The redesigned platform opens with a personalised summary dashboard — three key metrics, one chart, and a "What happened this week" feed that surfaces anomalies automatically. It answers the most important question before users have to ask it.

Deep analysis is one click away, not buried. The drill-down pattern is consistent across every section — users learn it once and it works everywhere. Power users retain full access to raw data and custom views; they just no longer have to start there.

Design Principle
"Every screen should answer one question. If a screen is trying to answer three questions, it's really answering none of them."
05 — Results & Learnings

The numbers, and what I'd do differently

Three months post-launch: DAU up 34%, support ticket volume down 28%, and the NPS score for the platform jumped 18 points. The customer success team reported that renewal conversations were markedly easier — customers could now demonstrate the value of the platform from the dashboard alone.

If I were doing this again, I'd push harder to get engineers involved in the research phase. Some of the most impactful "intelligent defaults" features were delayed because they required backend work we hadn't scoped early enough. Better alignment upfront would have shipped a more complete v1.
